Teaching Kids Gardening.

Children can learn important life skills while also developing a love of nature and a sense of responsibility through gardening. Here are some reasons to think about encouraging children to garden and some plants they might want to plant:

  1. Watching their tomato plants develop and produce fruit provides children with an opportunity to learn about trellising, pruning, and the pollination process.
  2. When they pull out a carrot they grew from seed, they might learn about root systems and be astounded.
  3. Children can take pleasure in the aromas, discover various flavours, and utilise them in cooking activities.
  4. Kids can quickly observe the complete lifespan, from seed germination to harvest.
  5. Kids can have fun watching the plants grow and fill the world with tasty berries.
  6. They can watch the upward-growing vines up close and learn about the value of supporting structures.

Crops for kids.

Tomatoes Plant

Duration: 60-80 days

Season: Early Spring

Process:

Six to eight weeks before the final anticipated date of frost in your location, start tomatoes from seeds indoors. Once all threat of frost has passed, put seedlings in well-prepared soil rich in organic materials. Place the plants at a distance of 2-3 feet (0.6-0.9 metres). Give the plants something to stand on, such cages or poles, to help them grow straight. Regular watering and adequate sun are essential for tomatoes. Mulch the area around the plants to keep moisture in and weeds out. To promote greater ventilation and fruit production, prune suckers (side shoots) that appear between the main stem and branches. Tomatoes should be picked when they are completely coloured and just a little bit hard to the touch.

Carrot

Duration: 60-80 days

Season: Early Spring

Process:

Pick a spot with full sun exposure and well-drained soil. Carrot seeds should be immediately sown into the ground at a depth of 1/4 to 1/2 inch (0.6 to 1.3 cm) and a distance of 2-3 inches (5 to 7.5 cm). During germination, keep the soil continually moist. Thin the seedlings to promote healthy root growth. Water the soil frequently to keep it moist but not soggy. Mulch helps weeds grow less by retaining moisture. Carrots should be harvested when the tops are the right size and the roots are a brilliant colour.

Radishes

Duration: 60-80 days

Season: Early Spring

Process:

Pick a spot that receives both full light and some shade. Directly plant radish seeds into well-prepared soil at a depth of about 1/2 inch (1.3 cm), spacing them 1-2 inches (2.5-5 cm) apart. Maintain a constant moisture level in the soil while plants are growing. To provide the seedlings enough room for root development, thin them. Water frequently to keep the soil moist. Use mulch to keep moisture in the soil and stop weed growth. When radishes reach the right size, harvest them by gently removing them out of the ground.

Grean-beans

Duration: 60-80 days

Season: Early Spring

Process:

Pick a spot that receives full sun. Green bean seeds should be directly sown 4-6 inches (10-15 cm) apart in well-drained soil at a depth of 1-2 inches (2.5-5 cm). Support climbing plant varieties. Water frequently, especially when the plant is blossoming or developing pods. Mulch helps keep soil wet and keeps weeds at bay. Green beans should be picked when their pods are fully developed but still immature, crisp, and tender.

Pumpkins:

Duration: 75-120 days

Season:seed sown indoors in spring

Process:

Select a sunny area with lots of room for the pumpkin vines to grow. By introducing compost or well rotted manure, you can prepare the soil. Depending on the cultivar, sow pumpkin seeds 3-5 feet (0.9-1.5 metres) apart and 1-2 inches (2.5-5 cm) deep. After planting, give the seedlings some water to keep the soil consistently moist. Support the fruit with your presence. Water frequently, especially as the fruit develops. Mulch helps weeds grow less by retaining moisture. Harvest pumpkins when their rinds get hard, their stalks become dry, and their colours are fully developed.

Blueberries

Duration: 70 to 100 days

Season: Early Spring

Process:

Choose a sunny location with acidic, well-drained soil if you want to cultivate blueberries. Eliminate weeds from the soil and add organic material, such as compost or peat moss. Depending on the variety, plant blueberries in late winter or early spring. Maintain a constant moisture level in the soil by regularly watering it with 1 to 2 inches of water per week. Apply an organic mulch layer all around the plants to keep moisture in and weed growth out of control. Remove any dead or damaged wood from the bushes by doing a late winter or early spring pruning. Use the right preventative and treatment steps to keep an eye out for pests and diseases. Keep in mind that blueberry bushes may take 2 to 4 years to reach maximum fruit output, so be patient and give them regular care.

Watermelon

Duration: 60-80 days

Season: Early Spring

Process:

Watermelons should be grown in a sunny area with well-drained soil. After the last frost, when the soil temperature reaches 70°F (21°C), sow seeds or seedlings. Plants need 3 to 4 feet between them. Offer 1-2 inches of steady watering per week, along with mulch to help plants retain moisture. Apply balanced fertiliser and limit your nitrogen intake. Observe for illnesses and pests. Plant flowers nearby to attract pollinators. Watermelons should be picked when they are fully grown, the underside is a creamy yellow colour, and the skin is dull. Cut the vine while still keeping a short stem intact. In the summer, savour the sweet, juicy benefits of homegrown watermelons.

Spinach

Duration: 40-50 days

Season: early spring or in the fall

Process:

Depending on the variety, sow spinach seeds directly into well-drained soil at a depth of about 12 inch (1.3 cm) and a distance of 6 to 12 inches (15 to 30 cm). To keep the plants from bolting (going to seed) too soon in hot weather, provide them with some partial shade or cool sun exposure. Maintain a constant moisture level in the soil that is not wet. Mulch helps weeds grow less by retaining moisture. Harvest outer leaves frequently once they are large enough to be useful, allowing the interior leaves to develop more. Spinach is good for planting in the early spring and the fall since it can withstand light frosts.

Chives

Duration: 30-60 days

Season: early spring or fall

Process:

Chives should be grown in an area with well-drained soil, sun, or light shade. Put seeds in the ground 1/4 inch deep, or space young plants 6 to 12 inches apart. For germination, keep the soil continually moist. Chives should be taken care of by keeping a consistent moisture level and lightly using organic fertiliser. When plants are 6 inches tall, harvest the leaves, allowing 2 inches for regrowth. In colder areas, use mulch as protection. Digging, dividing, and replanting clumps every few years will promote healthy development.

Strawberries

Duration: 4 - 6 weeks

Season: early spring or late summer to early fall.

Process:

Pick a spot with full sun exposure and well-drained soil. Organic matter should be added to the soil to prepare it. Make sure the strawberry crown is level with the soil surface before planting strawberry crowns or bare-root plants. Plants should be arranged in rows with 2-3 feet (0.6-0.9 metres) between rows and spaced 12-18 inches (30-45 cm) apart. After planting, give the plants plenty of moisture and keep the soil consistently moist. Mulch the area around the plants to keep weeds at bay and retain moisture. Strawberries should be picked when fully coloured and at the right sweetness, typically when they are fully red.
