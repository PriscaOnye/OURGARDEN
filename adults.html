Gardening crops to grow in difernt season.

Understanding the particular requirements and environmental factors of each season is necessary for growing vegetable crops in the garden. Prepare the soil in the spring, start seeds indoors, and gradually move them outside. Regular watering, mulching, and insect inspection are necessary during the summer. Harvesting mature crops, sowing cold-tolerant seeds, and safeguarding crops from frost are all necessary in the fall. Winter calls for seed beginning indoors, preparing for the following season, and making sure tools and materials are stored properly. Understanding crop rotation, soil fertility, and pest control is crucial all year round. Gardeners can reap a plentiful crop all year long by adjusting their methods to the shifting seasons.

Season Crops

Cabbage

Duration: 70 to 90 days

Season:early spring or fall

Process:

Start by adding compost to a well-drained soil. Ensure adequate spacing when sowing cabbage seeds in seed trays or directly into the ground. Maintain adequate soil moisture and sunlight. Thin out the seedlings as they develop to create adequate room for the heads to develop. Keep an eye out for diseases and pests, and take the necessary precautions to control them. When the cabbage heads are the right size and firmness, harvest them.

Sweet Potato

Duration:90 to 150 days

Season:late spring or early summer

Process:

Buy sweet potato slips (young plants) to get started, or grow your own from mature sweet potatoes. Prepare an organically rich, loose soil that drains nicely. Keep the soil moist while planting the slips in ridges or mounds. Regularly weed the area, and give it plenty of sunlight. Train the vines along the trellises or the ground as the plants mature. Sweet potatoes should be harvested when the foliage begins to yellow and wither. Before storing, treat the gathered sweet potatoes by letting them dry for 10 to 14 days in a warm, dry environment.

Cowpea Plant

Duration:70 to 90 days

Season: late spring or early summer

Process:

Loosen the soil and get rid of the weeds to prepare it. Cowpea seeds should be sown directly into the ground, with the correct spacing. Give the seeds plenty of sunlight and good irrigation. Cowpeas require little upkeep, although pests and illnesses should be watched out for. When the cowpea pods are mature but still delicate, they should be harvested. Leave the pods on the plants until they are dry and become brown if you want dried cowpeas.

Okra Plant

Duration:50 to 65 days

Season: late spring or early summer.

Process:

Prepare a well-drained soil and add organic stuff to it. Okra seeds should be directly planted, with adequate distance between each one. Give the seeds plenty of water and ample light exposure. If required, give the plants support as they grow. Keep the area clean of weeds and keep an eye out for insects like aphids and spider mites. Okra pods should be harvested when they are the correct length, which is typically 2 to 4 inches. Harvest regularly to promote ongoing production.

Groundnut (Peanut)

Duration:90 to 120 days

Season:late spring or early summer.

Process:

Pick a fertile, well-drained soil for your garden. Directly sow the groundnut seeds into the soil, giving them the appropriate spacing. Give the seeds plenty of water and ample light exposure. Make sure the plants have enough room to develop the underground peanut pods as they swell. Keep an eye out for insects and illnesses, especially fungi that can harm the leaves and pods. When the foliage begins to yellow and wither, harvest the groundnuts. Before extracting the peanuts from their shells, dig up the plants and let them dry for a few weeks in a warm, dry location.

Peppers

Duration: 60 to 90 days

Season: late spring or early summer

Process:

Pick a site with well-drained soil that is sunny. Purchase tiny seedlings of peppers or start pepper seeds indoors. After the risk of frost has passed, place the seedlings in the ground. For optimal airflow, the plants should be correctly spaced. Regularly water the plants, and add mulch to retain moisture. Keep an eye out for pests like pepper maggots or aphids and take the necessary precautions to control them. When the peppers are the right size and colour, harvest them. You can either select them while they are still green or wait for them to ripen and develop colour.

Eggplant Plant

Duration:60 to 90 days

Season: late spring or early summer

Process:

Prepare an organic rich, well-drained soil first. Purchase fresh seedlings of eggplant or start eggplant seeds indoors. After the dirt has warmed up, place the seedlings there. For optimal airflow, the plants should be correctly spaced. Allow for ample sun exposure and consistent watering. Mulch the area around the plants to keep moisture in and weeds out. Keep an eye out for pests like aphids or flea beetles and take the necessary steps to control them. When eggplants are the correct size and glossy, they are ready to be harvested.

Maize (Corn)

Duration: 70 to 90 days.

Season: late spring or early summer.

Process:

By introducing compost or well rotted manure, you can prepare the soil. Directly sow maize seeds into the soil, giving them the appropriate spacing. Since maize is wind-pollinated, planting should be done in blocks or numerous rows to maximise pollination. Regularly water the plants, especially during dry periods. Keep an eye out for pests like armyworms or maize borers, and take the necessary steps to control them. Baby corn should be harvested when the ears are still soft and between 4 and 6 inches long. When the sweet corn has fully formed but the milky stage is still there, harvest it.
